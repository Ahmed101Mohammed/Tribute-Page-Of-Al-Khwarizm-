Muhammad ibn Mūsa Al-Khwarizmī

Al-Khwarizmi is one of the greatest scientists throughout history, and he is the founder of algebra. Algorithms is attributed to him: a method for solving problems.

The time line of Al-Khwarizmi life:

781 CE

Al-Khwarizmi was born in 781 AD in Uzbekistan

825 CE

He wrote the Book of Addition and Differentiation on the Account of India in 825 CE, where he was primarily responsible for spreading the Indian numbering system throughout the Middle East and Europe.

830 CE

(Al-Kitab al-Mukhtasar fi al-Jabr wa al-Muqabala) is a mathematical book written by al-Khwarizmi around the year 830 CE. The term algebra is derived from the name of one of the basic operations with equations described in this book.

833 CE

Al-Khwarizmi has a book called Image of the Earth. Finished writing it in 833 AD. This book is a revised and complete version of Claudius Ptolemy's Geography.

847 CE

Muhammad Ibn Abdullah al-Khwarizmi died in 847 CE.
